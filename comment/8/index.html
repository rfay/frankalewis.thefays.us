Frank A Lewis: In Memoriam

Frank A Lewis, 1928-2011

Submitted by Nancy Lewis on Sun, 09/04/2011 - 13:38
Francis A. "Frank" Lewis of Weston, MA, formerly of Naples Florida and Cleveland, Ohio died peacefully September 3, 2011 at the age of 83.


Everyone loved my dad Frank because he was kind and always had a smile and a way to share his love of life. Even in his dying days he came out of a daze to crack a joke.

He had his naughty moments when he was a young, stubborn Scotsman, but as he grew older his temperament mellowed, his smart mind and tongue giving way to compassion and patience.

He cared so deeply and so completely for Barbara in her failing years. Upon retiring from the corporate world he became a full-engaged caretaker. I saw my dad take over in the kitchen and then in every other part of the household as Mom could no longer manage. For years he devoted himself completely to her care, managing the pills and the doctor visits. I guess this was training for his own future challenges.

The most important legacy he left was his long and loving partnership with the special woman of his life. Together they brought up a family of honest, hardworking, loyal and deeply caring children who in return passed on these qualities to their children and grandchildren: four children, seven grandchildren, and two great-grandchildren.

I will always remember my dad, Francis Lewis, Frankie Dutch, Featy, Dad, Grampy.

When it was his time to go he was efficient about it as always. He put the leaving-time on his calendar, took care of his business, and went peacefully to sleep.

I knew the end had come when he drank his last glass of wine and turned off Fox News for the first time.

I don't know where he is now, but I don't think he really cared but as long as it was with Mom.

I know that twinkle in his eyes will always continue. I will always look up to the twinkling stars and know that one of those is my dad's love shining down on us all.

It's hard to think about

It's hard to think about Frank and not have a smile on your face. Frank and Barbara were our neighbors and friends at Royal Wood Golf and Country Club in Naples, FL. Barb and I called our hubbies Frick and Frack. They always had one project or another going on and we knew it was serious business when the tool belts came out. Frank had TWO...one suede, one leather (I think they weighed more than he did). Between the two of them, there was no home project that couldn't be mastered. Frank was a wonderful husband. He was a real 'family man' who spoke of his children and their families with lots of pride and joy. He was also a great friend with that 'twinkle in his eye' that we remember so well. Thank you, Frank, for touching our lives. We will never forget you. Love, Jet and Ron
